Precision is important when authoring standards and other consequential documents. Since problem domains and terminologies are constantly evolving, accurate authoring and interpretation of documents is difficult without a shared vocabulary.

Maintaining a vocabulary involves many challenges, including tracking up-to-date definitions and preferred term expressions, managing relationships between concepts, and making the vocabulary database continuously accessible by authors and readers.

Dissatisfied by how general-purpose tools such as wikis and spreadsheets handle these challenges, and inspired by vocabulary management workflows at ISO, we’ve built a special-purpose open solution that embodies those workflows.

What is Glossarist and why use it?

Glossarist is a suite of open software for managing the vocabulary and for exposing that vocabulary via accessible human- and machine-friendly interfaces.

Underlying that software is a framework for thinking about vocabulary lifecycle and evolution that was inspired by ISO terminology management processes.

The Glossarist framework complies with the principles of terminology organization described in ISO 704:2009, and facilitates terminology management in accordance with ISO 10241-1:2011.

With Glossarist, vocabulary curators can:

  • Manage concept hierarchy and inter-concept relationships.
  • Keep track of each concept’s preferred terms in multiple languages.
  • Register lifecycle events that affect obsolete/preferred statuses for terms and concept definitions.
  • Enable vocabulary users to efficiently explore the vocabulary.
  • Enable vocabulary contributors to propose changes to term expressions or definitions.

Subsequently, document authors and readers can:

  • Quickly locate the most accurate and up-to-date term to express a concept in a given language.
  • Quickly locate current definitions for unfamiliar terms and disambiguate meaning.
  • Explore adjacent concepts relevant to the topic at hand.
  • Track down definitions of outdated terms when reading documents authored in the past.
